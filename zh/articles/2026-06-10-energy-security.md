# Iran Crisis Exposes Energy Vulnerability: Clean Power Offers Protection

The US-Israel conflict with Iran has sent fossil fuel prices soaring, exposing the profound vulnerability of economies dependent on oil and gas imports. Analysis suggests UK energy bills could rise dramatically under prolonged disruption, with estimates ranging from 900 to 2500 pounds annually per household. Green energy investments are increasingly framed as an insurance policy against geopolitical risk rather than purely environmental measures. More North Sea drilling cannot shield households from global price volatility in interconnected energy markets. The economic case for domestic renewable generation strengthens with each geopolitical crisis. Offshore wind capacity in the North Sea represents a strategic asset for energy independence. Solar photovoltaic deployment on residential and commercial buildings reduces exposure to wholesale electricity price spikes. Energy efficiency programs provide immediate bill relief while reducing overall demand. The International Energy Agency has repeatedly documented that clean energy transitions enhance energy security. Diversification of energy sources, including nuclear and hydrogen, further reduces vulnerability to supply disruptions.
