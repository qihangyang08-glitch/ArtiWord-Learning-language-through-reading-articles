# The Green Transition: Politicians Should Speed It Up

Political divisions over energy policy are threatening the pace of the green transition. As elections approach, Conservative parties are drifting from net zero commitments while populist movements openly oppose renewable energy deployment. India and European leaders are pushing toward faster transition following fuel price shocks that exposed vulnerabilities in fossil fuel dependence. The economics of renewable energy have shifted decisively: solar installations reached record levels globally, and offshore wind projects are delivering power at costs below natural gas. Grid modernization remains the critical bottleneck for integrating variable renewable sources. Energy storage technologies, particularly lithium-ion batteries and green hydrogen, are seeing rapid cost reductions. Carbon border adjustment mechanisms are being debated as tools to prevent carbon leakage while protecting domestic industries. The social dimensions of transition, including job creation in clean energy sectors and just transition funds for fossil fuel workers, are central to maintaining political support. Climate adaptation costs are rising faster than projected, adding urgency to mitigation efforts.
